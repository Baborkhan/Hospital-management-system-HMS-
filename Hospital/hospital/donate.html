Misconceptions

Myths vs Facts

❌ Myth

Doctors won't try hard to save me if I'm a registered donor.

✅ Fact

Donor status has zero effect on your care. The transplant team is entirely separate from treating physicians.

❌ Myth

Organ donation is haram in Islam.

✅ Fact

Islamic Fiqh Academy (OIC 1988), Darul Ifta Bangladesh, and the majority of contemporary scholars declared voluntary unpaid donation permissible (halal) — Sadaqah Jariyah.

❌ Myth

Only young healthy people can donate.

✅ Fact

People of any age can register. Suitability is assessed medically at the time of death.

❌ Myth

MedFind will sell or share my information.

✅ Fact

Your data is encrypted, stored only for donor records, never shared or sold. Withdraw anytime.

Official Scholarly Rulings (Fatawa) — Summary:

1. Islamic Fiqh Academy — OIC, Jeddah (1988): Resolution No. 26 (1/4) formally declared voluntary, unpaid organ donation permissible in Islam under four conditions: (a) donor's free and informed consent, (b) no financial payment involved, (c) donor's life not endangered, (d) performed by qualified medical professionals.

2. Darul Ifta Bangladesh: Issued fatwa permitting voluntary organ donation with family consent, citing Hifzul-Nafs as one of the five Maqasid of Islamic Sharia.

3. IMAB & IMANA: Both organisations support voluntary organ donation as consistent with Islamic values.

4. Scholarly majority: Contemporary Hanafi, Shafi'i, Maliki, and Hanbali scholars concur on permissibility.
