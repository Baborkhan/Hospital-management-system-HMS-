What is Organ Donation?

How It Works

Organ donation is the gift of an organ or tissue to help someone who needs a transplant. It is completely voluntary, free, and done through licensed medical authorities only.

📝
Register Your Decision
You sign up as a voluntary donor. Your family is informed. No organ is taken during your lifetime — only after confirmed brain death or cardiac death, with family consent.
👨‍👩‍👧
Family Consent is Key
In Bangladesh, family consent is legally and ethically essential. Your family must agree. Informing them of your decision now makes this process smoother.
🏥
Hospital Handles Everything
A licensed hospital or transplant authority manages the entire process under strict medical and legal supervision. MedFind only facilitates awareness and registration.
🫀
What Can Be Donated?
Kidneys, liver, heart, lungs, corneas, pancreas, intestines, bone marrow, skin, and more. A single donor can give life to 8+ people and restore sight to 2 more.
⚖️
Completely Legal
Governed by Transplantation of Human Organs Act 1999 (Bangladesh). Buying or selling organs is illegal. All donations must be voluntary and through authorized channels.
🔒
Your Data is Safe
Your registration data is encrypted, never sold or publicly shared. Only you and authorized medical personnel can access your donor information.

Common Misconceptions

Myths vs Facts

❌ Myth

Doctors won't try as hard to save me if I'm a registered donor.

✅ Fact

Donor status has zero impact on your treatment. Transplant teams are completely separate from emergency medical teams.

❌ Myth

Organ donation is against Islam / religion.

✅ Fact

Most Islamic scholars consider organ donation permissible (halal) as it saves lives. It is viewed as an act of great charity (sadaqah).

❌ Myth

Only young, healthy people can donate organs.

✅ Fact

People of all ages can donate. Medical professionals decide at time of death which organs are suitable. Even 70+ year olds have donated life-saving corneas.

❌ Myth

You can buy or sell organs through platforms like MedFind.

✅ Fact

Buying/selling organs is strictly illegal in Bangladesh (punishable by law). MedFind only facilitates awareness and voluntary registration. No transactions ever.

☪️ Islamic Perspective on Organ Donation
The Islamic Fiqh Council and most contemporary scholars permit organ donation on the basis of saving lives (Hifzul-Nafs), which is one of the five objectives of Islamic law. The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) said: "Whoever saves one life, it is as if he has saved all of mankind" (Quran 5:32). Donating organs is considered a form of ongoing charity (Sadaqah Jariyah) that continues to benefit others even after death. Families should consult their local scholars if they have specific concerns.
