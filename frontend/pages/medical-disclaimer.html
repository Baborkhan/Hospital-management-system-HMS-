Organ & Tissue Donation
Registration, consent, legal framework, and medical limitations
Organ donation is a profound, voluntary act of humanity that can save up to 8 lives and restore sight or function for up to 10 additional individuals through tissue donation. MedFind supports organ donation awareness in alignment with Bangladesh's Human Organ Transplantation Act, 1999 (amended 2018) and the guidelines of the Directorate General of Health Services (DGHS). Please read the following carefully before registering or making any decisions regarding organ donation.
Donatable Organs
  • Heart, lungs, liver, kidneys (both)
  • Pancreas, small intestine
  • Corneas, skin, bone, heart valves
  • Tendons, cartilage, blood vessels
Who Can Register
  • Any person aged 18 years or older
  • Sound mental capacity at time of consent
  • No requirement of perfect health
  • Family notification is strongly recommended
Important Limitations
  • Most transplants require brain-death certification by a medical panel
  • Suitability of organs is determined at time of death — not at registration
  • Medical teams may not be able to honour all donor wishes
  • Living donation is only legally permitted between closely related kin
Legal Framework (Bangladesh)
  • Human Organ Transplantation Act, 1999
  • Amendment Act, 2018 — expanded donor pool
  • DGHS-approved transplant hospitals only
  • Commercial organ trading is a criminal offence
  • Registration on MedFind constitutes an expression of intent only — it is not a legally binding donor card under Bangladeshi law; always carry a signed donor consent and inform family members
  • MedFind does not coordinate actual organ retrieval, transplantation, or matching — these are exclusively managed by DGHS-approved transplant hospitals and the National Organ Transplant Programme
  • MedFind does not verify medical suitability of registered donors — final determination is made by the transplant medical team at the time of donation
  • Donors retain the full right to withdraw consent at any time by contacting us at medfindbd2026@gmail.com or by updating their profile
  • Family members (next of kin) have legal authority to consent or refuse donation in Bangladesh regardless of the deceased's registered wishes — discussing your decision with family is critically important
  • No commercial benefit may be offered or received in connection with organ donation — MedFind strictly prohibits any such transactions on the platform
  • Religious and cultural beliefs are deeply personal — MedFind does not advocate any specific religious position on organ donation; we encourage users to seek guidance from their religious authority
  • Tissue donation (corneas, skin, bone) follows different protocols than organ donation — contact your nearest eye bank or tissue bank for specific guidance

For official organ donor registration, transplant hospital listings, and the National Organ Transplant Programme, contact the Directorate General of Health Services (DGHS) at dghs.gov.bd or call the health helpline 16167. MedFind's donor registry is supplementary and informational only.
